PRESENTATION

Tiramisu is one of the cornerstones of Italian cuisine, one of the most beloved and widely made spoon desserts worldwide. The origins of tiramisu are highly uncertain, with different cities claiming its paternity. What we are certain of is that since 1980, the year this term was added to the Italian vocabulary, tiramisu has enjoyed tremendous success! Today, it is among the most recognized Italian words abroad. But what makes tiramisu so special? Certainly, we love the fact that it is very easy to prepare, and its mascarpone cream is irresistible—so tasty and velvety, interspersed with layers of ladyfingers soaked in coffee! Some prefer to flavor the soaking liquid with Marsala, while others opt for different liqueurs. However, we love it just the way it is, in its pure form, and we present to you the most classic version, perfect for any occasion! Even though we haven't definitively identified the inventor of the current version of tiramisu—some attribute it to Ado Campeol (the owner of the well-known and historic establishment "Il Becchiere" in Treviso)—we share with you our recipe for one of the best tiramisus you will ever taste! Give it a try and let us know!
